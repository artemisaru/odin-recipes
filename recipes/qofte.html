Baked Mint Meatballs(Qofte)

Description

Albanian Mint Meatballs with ground beef (or qofte) are a version of kofta. Koftas are known throughout Mediterranean, the Balkans, Middle East etc.

They are usually made with ground beef, however there are versions with lamb, chicken, vegetables etc. The most common method of cooking the meatballs is to fry them. Albanian meatballs are one of the most versatile dishes you can make. Typically they are served with a big salad, a yogurt based dip, bread etc. However, you can also serve these mint meatballs with spaghetti, either on their own or in a tomato based sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs Ground Beef
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 tbsp Olive Oil
  • Parsley
  • 7-8 Mint Leaves
  • 1 Red Onion
  • 1/2 cup Bread Crumbs
  • 5 Garlic Gloves
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Black Pepper

Steps

  1. Mince herbs

    Mince garlic, chop onion, chop parsley and mint.

  2. Prepare the mixture

    Mix thawed ground beef, eggs, olive oil, garlic, onion, bread crumbs, salt, pepper, parsley and mint together. Mix thoroughly until you create a dough like substance. Cover bowl with plastic.

  3. Preheat oven

    Refrigerate for 1 hour to let the flavors really mix together. Preheat oven to 400 degreed Fahrenheit

  4. Shape the meatballs

    Create small balls with your hands out of the dough. Line them up in a baking sheet. I prefer using parchment paper on the baking sheet.

  5. Bake and serve

    Bake for about 40 minutes. Let cool and serve with salad and your side of choice.
